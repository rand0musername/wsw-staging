It was like my mind was a repository for all of the memories of the world, and I was the only one who could access them. Sometimes it was overwhelming, like trying to dig through a box of old photographs and memorabilia from a life that wasn't even mine. But mostly it was a gift, a way for me to connect with people and places from the past in a way that most people could only dream of. My cousins looked at me in awe as I described every moment in vivid detail, and I realized that I had a gift that was far beyond the reach of most people. And the more I explored that repository, the more I realized that the memories of the world were not just the collective experiences of the past, but the collective hope for the future as well. Sometimes I felt like the weight of the past was crushing me, and the future was a distant, uncertain thing that I could barely perceive. But mostly it was a gift, an opportunity to connect with people and places from the past in a way that most people could only dream of. Now, as I sit in my living room, surrounded by the trappings of a life well-lived, I realize that the memories I have retrieved are not just the experiences of the past, but the foundations of the future as well.
